https://stackoverflow.com/questions/952914/how-do-i-make-a-flat-list-out-of-a-list-of-lists


	In the link provided, a user is asking how to flatten out a list of lists. The idea comes by having elements within the lists inside the list that a programmer would like to have access to by only using a single list. While it sounds complex in nature and can be quite entertaining to solve, I don’t believe stack overflow would be the right place to ask such a thing.
I would consider this a bad post due to the fact that the complexity of the question is far from the types of questions being asked on the rest of the website. With the tutorials of python, one could deduce how to solve the question presented by the original poster. This would violate many rules found in the guide written by Raymond and Moen. 
While this question may have a lot of votes and may seem like a well liked question, that doesn’t stop the fact that the poster did not go over other methods of solving the problem before posting it on the forum. There is no indication that they even attempted to solve it, nor did they mention a thought process of what got them stuck on the issue in the first place. 
The only caveat is that we must give the benefit of the doubt that coding question etiquette had not been established by the time the question was posted as this was dated back 14 years ago.

https://stackoverflow.com/questions/77046536/split-numpy-array-into-segments-where-condition-is-met


	Is a different post where it definitely shows a lot more effort put in by the original poster when it comes to attempting to find the solution. In this problem the OP has an array of integers and is looking for a clean answer to getting rid of all the negative numbers, while also splitting the array into smaller arrays whenever a negative number is encountered. Thus the result should be multiple arrays of positive integers. 
	In the latter part of the post, the OP shows that they understand how to solve this already through a different method but is looking for something that would reduce runtime on larger data sets by lowering the amount of computations needed to solve this. He also links an answer from geeksforgeeks that shows the split action they would like to use at the negative numbers but wants to optimize it so they can simultaneously be removed. 
	Much of this post follows the guidelines set by Raymond and Moen and showcases an effort put into attempting to solve the problem beforehand.  In response, there were many solutions put forth in the comments, many of which had clean and short answers, which is what OP was aiming for.
